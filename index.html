01 / Case Study

Scheduler & Ops Platform

Commercial Contractor  ·  Cleveland, Ohio

ReactNode.jsFirebaseRealtime DB

The problem

Scheduling ran on a fragmented mix of manual calendars, phone calls, and email threads. No single source of truth — field crews, billing, and management were each working from different information.

What was built

  • Real-time calendar with color-coded job types
  • Employee assignment and field crew visibility
  • Role-based access from field to management

The result

The calendar became the company's single source of truth — replacing the patchwork entirely. Any administrator can manage it without touching code.

Live platform — real-time calendar with color-coded job types

The problem

Sales activity was untracked and inconsistent. No structured outreach, no research framework, no visibility into pipeline status.

What was built

  • Full CRM with staged pipeline and lead detail views
  • SOP-driven workflow from first touch to close
  • Activity tracking, task management, and file storage

The result

Sales moved from informal and reactive to structured and repeatable. The same CRM that was built is the one actively used to manage the pipeline today.

Live platform — CRM lead detail with activity timeline and stage tracking

The problem

Equipment staging was manual and error-prone. Supply inventory had no automated awareness — items were over-purchased or missing.

What was built

  • Equipment database with job-specific automated staging
  • Inventory tracking across 50+ line items
  • Shared data layer with scheduler and CRM

The result

Manual prep coordination was eliminated. Over-purchasing stopped. Because every module shares the same data layer, adding inventory required no rearchitecting.

Live platform — job creation with type classification and crew assignment
